# University of Michigan AP Credit Guidelines (2026)

This document outlines how AP exam scores translate to course credit, credit hours, and placement at the University of Michigan. Use this to determine what requirements a student has satisfied and what courses they can enroll in next.

## General Rules

- **Ross School of Business** first-year students receive credit according to LSA guidelines, with a maximum of 30 credit hours completed prior to the first year (inclusive of test and transfer credit) toward the BBA degree.
- Schools and colleges not explicitly listed award credit according to **LSA guidelines** by default.
- The College Board institution code for U-M is **1839**.

---

## College of Engineering

The College of Engineering awards credit according to LSA guidelines **unless specified below**. Key differences from LSA:

### Chemistry
- **Score 4 or 5**: CHEM 130 (3 cr) + CHEM 125/126 (1 cr each) = **5 credit hours**

### Computer Science
- **CS A, Score 5**: EECS 180 (4 cr), placed into **EECS 280**
- **CS Principles, Score 4 or 5**: EECS 101x departmental credit (4 cr)

### Math
- **Calculus AB, Score 5**: Mathematics 120/115 (4 cr)
- **Calculus BC, Score 4**: Mathematics 120/115 (4 cr)
- **Calculus BC, Score 5**: Mathematics 120/115 (4 cr) + Mathematics 121/116 (4 cr) = **8 credit hours**
- Engineering does **not** award credit for the AB subscore of the BC exam.

### Physics
- **Physics C: Mechanics, Score 5**: Physics 140 (4 cr) + 141 (1 cr) = **5 credit hours**
- **Physics C: E&M, Score 5**: Physics 240 (4 cr) + 241 (1 cr) = **5 credit hours**
- Engineering does **not** award credit for AP Physics B, AP Physics 1, or AP Physics 2.

## College of Literature, Science & the Arts (LSA)

This is the default credit policy for most schools/colleges.

### Biology
- **Score 3**: Biology 100 (4 cr) → eligible for Bio 171 or 172
- **Score 4**: Biology 195 (5 cr) → eligible for any course requiring Bio 171 or 172 as a prerequisite
- **Score 5**: Biology 195 (5 cr) + Biology 196 (1 cr) = **6 credit hours** → eligible for any course requiring Bio 173 as a prerequisite *(effective summer 2026 incoming class)*

### Chemistry
- **Score 4 or 5**: CHEM 130 (3 cr) + CHEM 125/126 (1 cr each) = **5 credit hours** → placed into CHEM 210 or 211
- Students pursuing pre-health or graduate science fields should consult the LSA Basic Pre-Health Science Classes webpage for exemption letter info.

### Chinese
- No direct AP credit. Students take the UM Chinese Language Program proficiency test for placement or to satisfy the 4-semester language requirement.

### Computer Science
- **CS A, Score 5**: EECS 180 (4 cr) → eligible for EECS 280
- **CS Principles, Score 4 or 5**: EECS 101x departmental credit (4 cr)

### Economics
- **Microeconomics, Score 4 or 5**: 2 cr departmental credit. **Does not satisfy ECON 101 requirements.**
- **Macroeconomics, Score 4 or 5**: 2 cr departmental credit. **Does not satisfy ECON 102 requirements.**

### English
- **Language and Composition, Score 4 or 5**: English 101x (3 cr)
- **Literature and Composition, Score 4 or 5**: English 101x (3 cr)
- Both exams together = 6 credits total.
- **Does not satisfy** the First Year Writing requirement or English major requirements. Counts only toward total graduation credits.

### Environmental Science
- **Score 4 or 5**: EARTH 219 (3 cr) + EARTH 218 (1 cr) = **4 credit hours**

### French
- **Score 4 or 5**: French 232 (4 cr) → placed into French 235. Satisfies LSA language requirement.
- 3 additional credits for French 250x awarded upon completing French 235 or RCLANG 320 with a B or better. French 250x counts toward the French major/minor as a 270-level course.

### Geography
- **Human Geography, Score 4 or 5**: 3 cr departmental credit

### German
- **Score 3**: German 231 (4 cr) → placed into 232
- **Score 4**: German 231 (4 cr) → placed into German 300 and many upper-level courses. Students strongly advised to enroll in German 300. 4 additional credits for German 232 awarded upon completing an upper-level German course with a B or better.
- **Score 5**: German 232 (4 cr) → placed into German 300 and upper-level courses. Satisfies language requirement. 4 additional credits for German 301X awarded upon completing an upper-level course with a B or better.
- Students may receive credit via AP **or** the departmental placement test, not both.

### History of Art
- **Score 4 or 5**: HISTART 201x (3 cr). Students planning a major/minor should consult a departmental concentration advisor.

### History
- **American, Score 4 or 5**: 100-level History (4 cr)
- **European, Score 4 or 5**: 100-level History (4 cr)
- **World, Score 4 or 5**: 100-level History (4 cr)
- 100-level courses **do not count** toward the History major. An AP and IB exam in the same subject only count for one 100-level course.

### Italian
- **Score 4 or 5**: Italian 232 (4 cr) → eligible for any course beyond Italian 232. Satisfies language requirement.
- 3 additional credits for Italian 237X awarded upon completing one course beyond 232 (taught in Italian) with a B or better. Counts toward the Italian major/minor as an upper 200-level course.

### Japanese
- No direct AP credit. Students take an orientation placement test for course placement.

### Latin
- **Vergil, Score 4 or 5**: 200-level Latin (4 cr). Students should take the placement exam for appropriate course placement or to satisfy the language requirement.

### Mathematics
- **Calculus AB, Score 5**: Mathematics 120 (2 cr) → placed into Math 116, 156, 185, 186, 275, 276, or 295. 2 additional credits granted upon successful completion of one of those courses with a C or better.
- **Calculus BC, Score 4**: Mathematics 120 (2 cr) → same placement and additional credit rules as AB Score 5.
- **Calculus BC, Score 5**: Mathematics 120 (2 cr) + Mathematics 121 (2 cr) = **4 credit hours** → placed into Math 116, 156, 185, 186, 205, 214, 215, 216, 217, 285, 286, 275, 276, 295, 417, or 419. 2 additional credits for each of Math 120 and 121 granted upon completing one of these courses with a C or better.
- No credit for Math 120 or 121 if Math 105 or 115 is elected.
- The Math Department does **not** award credit for the AB subscore of the BC exam.
- Math 120 and 121 represent AP credit only; they are not actual U-M courses.
- The department strongly encourages honors courses (Math 156, 275, 185, or 295) for students with AP credit.

### Physics
- **Physics 1 + Physics 2, both Score 5**: Physics 125 (4 cr) + 127 (1 cr) + 126 (4 cr) + 128 (1 cr) = **10 credit hours**
- **Physics C: Mechanics, Score 5**: Physics 139 (5 cr — lecture/discussion 4, lab 1)
- **Physics C: E&M, Score 5**: Physics 239 (5 cr — lecture/discussion 4, lab 1)
- **Physics B, Score 4**: Physics 125 (4 cr) + 127 (1 cr) = **5 credit hours**
- **Physics B, Score 5**: Physics 125 (4 cr) + 127 (1 cr) + 126 (4 cr) + 128 (1 cr) = **10 credit hours**
- Note: AP Physics B was replaced by AP Physics 1 and 2 starting Fall 2014.

### Political Science
- **US Government & Politics, Score 4 or 5**: Political Science 111 (4 cr)
- **Comparative Government & Politics, Score 4 or 5**: Political Science 140 (4 cr)

### Psychology
- **Score 4 or 5**: Psychology 111 (4 cr) → eligible for any 200-level Psychology course

### Spanish
- **Language, Score 4 or 5**: Spanish 232 (4 cr) + Spanish 279x (up to 3 additional cr) → placed into Spanish 277. Satisfies language requirement. 3 additional credits for 279x awarded upon completing Spanish 277, 278, or RCLANG 324 with a B or better. 279x counts toward major/minor as a 279–399 level course.
- **Literature, Score 4 or 5**: Spanish 232 (4 cr) + Spanish 294x (up to 3 additional cr) → placed into Spanish 277. Satisfies language requirement. 3 additional credits for 294x awarded upon completing Spanish 277, 278, or RCLANG 324 with a B or better. 294x counts toward major/minor as a 279–399 level literature course.
- Credit can only be earned once for the language **or** literature test, not both.

### Statistics
- **Score 4 or 5**: Stats 180 (3 cr)

## Stamps School of Art & Design

Awards credit according to LSA guidelines unless specified below. Applicable to students admitted summer 2025 and after.

### 2-D Art and Design
- **Score 4 or 5**: ARTDES 101X (3 cr departmental) → eligible for ARTDES 115. Academic elective credit only; does not count as studio credit.

### 3-D Art and Design
- **Score 4 or 5**: ARTDES 101X (3 cr departmental) → eligible for ARTDES 120. Academic elective credit only; does not count as studio credit.

### Drawing
- **Score 4 or 5**: ARTDES 101X (3 cr departmental) → eligible for ARTDES 100 and 105. Academic elective credit only; does not count as studio credit.

- Each exam transfers as 3 credits. A total of **9 credits** can be earned across all three art exams.

## Key Caveats to Remember

1. **Economics AP credit does not replace ECON 101 or 102** — students still need to take those courses if required.
2. **English AP credit does not satisfy First Year Writing** — it only adds to total graduation credits.
3. **History 100-level AP credit does not count toward the History major.**
4. **Language AP credit** generally satisfies the LSA 4-semester language requirement when it grants credit for a 232-level course.
5. **Additional/bonus credits** for languages (French, German, Italian, Spanish) require completing a subsequent course with a grade of **B or better**.
6. **Math AP credit hours are provisional** — full credit for Math 120/121 requires completing a subsequent math course with a **C or better**. Electing Math 105 or 115 voids AP math credit entirely.
7. **Engineering has stricter physics requirements** — only Physics C exams with a score of 5 earn credit; Physics 1, 2, and B do not.
8. **Ross BBA** accepts a max of 30 pre-first-year credit hours (AP + transfer combined).
9. **Spanish** language and literature exams cannot both earn credit — only one counts.
