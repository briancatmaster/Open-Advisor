# University of Michigan – Ann Arbor: Undergraduate Graduation Requirements & AP/IB/Transfer Credit Guide
*Synthesized from official U-M sources. Verified March 2026. Always confirm with Wolverine Access degree audit and your academic advisor.*

---

## How to Use This as LLM Context

Requirements at U-M work as a **dependency graph**:
- College-wide rules (total credits, residency, GPA, distribution) define the outer space
- Major cores define ordering/sequencing
- AP/IB/transfer credits remove prerequisites and add elective buffer

The two most common planning mistakes: (1) having enough *credit hours* but not satisfying named *requirements*, and (2) assuming AP credit can substitute for in-residence distribution/writing/QR requirements when it often cannot.

## College of LSA (BA / BS / BGS)

### Credit Requirements
- **Total:** 120 credits minimum (124 for BS Chemistry)
- **LSA credits:** At least 100 of 120 must be LSA-designated courses
- **Graded credits:** At least 90 must be graded (letter grade, not P/F)
- **Residency:** At least 60/120 in-residence; at least 30 of the last 60 in-residence. AP/transfer/credit-by-exam counts as out-of-residence.
- **Non-LSA credit cap:** Max 20 non-LSA credits toward the 120; max 60 credits in any one department
- **GPA:** 2.0 cumulative; 2.0 in all major coursework (including prereqs, required courses, electives)

### General Education Requirements (All LSA Students)

| Requirement | How to Satisfy | AP/IB Eligible? | Transfer Credit? |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| First-Year Writing (FYWR) | One approved 4-credit course (e.g., English 125) | **NO** — AP English does NOT satisfy FYWR | Yes, with Sweetland approval |
| Upper-Level Writing (ULWR) | One approved upper-level course; C- or better; must satisfy FYWR first | NO | NO — must be in-residence |
| Race & Ethnicity (R&E) | One designated R&E course (3+ credits) | NO — test credit excluded; transfer petition allowed for credits earned before May 1, 2026 | Petition only (policy tightened May 2026) |
| Quantitative Reasoning (QR) | One QR/1 course OR two QR/2 courses; physics and most stats qualify | **NO AP for QR** — IB may qualify in limited cases | Yes if directly equivalent to a U-M QR course |
| Language | 4th-term proficiency via U-M exam or 4th-semester U-M course (C- or better) | **YES** — AP/IB language scores (4+) can satisfy this | Transfer credit for 4th-term equivalent works |
| Area Distribution (30 cr) | 7 credits each in Natural Science (NS), Social Science (SS), Humanities (HU) | **NO** — AP explicitly excluded from Distribution | Yes — transfer credit CAN fulfill distribution |
| Major | 2.0 GPA; max 60 credits per dept; no course can double-count for both major AND distribution | Test credit may satisfy prerequisites but not major requirements directly | Varies by department |

**Critical AP constraints for LSA:**
- AP credit CANNOT satisfy the Distribution Requirement (NS/SS/HU)
- AP credit CANNOT satisfy the QR Requirement
- AP credit CAN satisfy: language requirement, major prerequisites, general elective totals
- AP credit CANNOT satisfy FYWR or ULWR

### Major/Minor Universal Rules
- No course can simultaneously satisfy a major requirement AND the LSA Distribution Requirement (no double-counting)
- Test credits (AP/IB) cannot satisfy major/minor requirements — but may satisfy prerequisites (check with department)
- Honors majors require 3.4+ overall GPA
- Minors: at least 15 credits, at least 9 in-residence, no course shared between two minors, at most one course shared between a minor and your major, no P/F courses in a minor
- Pass/fail cannot be used for major or minor courses (post-Fall 2024 policy)

### Common LSA Majors — Key Requirements

**Economics**
- Prereqs: ECON 101 (Micro), ECON 102 (Macro), MATH 115
- Core: ECON 401, 402 (theory), ECON 405 (econometrics), 4 upper-level electives
- AP shortcuts: AP Micro/Macro (4+) can skip ECON 101/102; AP Calc AB/BC clears MATH 115

**Psychology**
- Prereqs: PSYCH 111, stats course (STATS 250 or equiv)
- Core: PSYCH 211 (research methods), 3 upper-level cognate areas, capstone lab
- AP shortcuts: AP Psych (4+) skips PSYCH 111; AP Stats may satisfy stat prereq

**Biology / EEB / MCDB**
- Prereqs: BIO 171 + 172, CHEM 130 + labs, MATH 115
- Core varies by track; lab-intensive; pre-med students typically also take orgo + physics
- AP shortcuts: AP Bio (4+) = BIO 171+172; AP Chem = CHEM 130; AP Calc AB = MATH 115

**Computer Science (LSA)**
- Prereqs: EECS 203, EECS 280, MATH 115, 116
- Core: EECS 281, 370, 376, 482; 4 upper-level tech electives; capstone
- AP shortcuts: AP CS A (4+) → EECS 285 placement; AP Calc BC (5) = both MATH 115+116

**Political Science**
- Prereqs: POLSCI 111, one methods course
- Core: 4 subfield courses, 1 upper-level seminar, POLSCI 395 (methods)

**Math / Statistics**
- Prereqs: MATH 115, 116, 215, 217 or 216
- Honors track available (MATH 295/296); STATS 425/426 for stats concentration
- AP shortcuts: AP Calc BC (5) = MATH 115+116; honors track (156/185) recommended for strong AP students

**Economics**
- Prereqs: ECON 101, 102, MATH 115
- Core: ECON 401, 402, 405, upper-level electives

## College of Engineering (BSE)

**128 total credits.** Students enter undeclared; must declare a major by start of 3rd term.

### Universal Core (Required for ALL BSE Degrees)

| Requirement | Courses / Credits | AP/IB Credit? |
|-------------|------------------|---------------|
| Mathematics | MATH 115 (4), 116 (4), 215 (4), 216 (4) = 16 cr | AP Calc AB (4+): MATH 115; AP Calc BC (5): MATH 115+116; BC (4): MATH 115 only |
| Physics | PHYS 140+141 (5 cr), PHYS 240+241 (5 cr) = 10 cr | AP Physics C Mech (5): PHYS 140+141; AP Physics C E&M (5): PHYS 240+241 |
| Chemistry | CHEM 125/126 + 130 (5 cr) OR CHEM 210+211 | AP Chemistry (4 or 5): core chemistry satisfied |
| ENGR 100 | 4 cr — writing-intensive; C or better required; must be in-residence | NO — transfer FYWR accepted only for transfer students |
| ENGR 101/151 | 4 cr — Python programming | AP CS A (4+): 2 credits toward EECS 285; check with advisor re: ENGR 101 substitution |
| Intellectual Breadth | 16 cr of Liberal Arts Courses (HU/SS-marked); ≥3 cr HU; ≥3 cr 300-level LAC | AP/IB CAN count for IB EXCEPT the 3-credit HU requirement; foreign lang AP (200+ level) counts as LAC |
| General Electives | Remaining credits to reach 128 | AP/IB can fill these |

**Engineering GPA/Residency Rules:**
- 2.0 GPA in all non-P/F CoE coursework
- 2.0 GPA in all courses within your academic department
- At least 50 credits of UM-AA coursework generating CTP (Credit Toward Program)
- At least 30 credits of 300+ level technical CoE courses generating CTP
- C-rule: C- or below in most required science/math/engineering courses is unsatisfactory; many programs require C or better
- No double-counting: one course cannot satisfy two requirements within a BSE program

### Major-Specific Requirements

**Computer Science**
- EECS 203 (Discrete Math), 280 (Prog+Data Structures), 281 (Algorithms), 370 (Computer Org), 376, 482 (OS)
- Stats: STATS 250 or EECS 301
- Major Design Experience (MDE)
- C or better in all non-elective requirements; C- rule applies

**Electrical Engineering**
- EECS 215 (Circuits), 216 (Signals+Systems), 270 (Logic Design), 280, 311, 312, 351 (Devices)
- Lab sequence; MDE
- EECS 215 requires PHYS 240 as prereq/co-req

**Computer Engineering**
- EECS 203, 215, 216, 270, 280, 370, 473, 482
- TCHNCLCM 300 + 496; MDE
- TCHNCLCM 496 must be same term or later than MDE

**Mechanical Engineering**
- ME 211, 240, 250, 320, 335, 360, 382, 395, 450, 495
- EECS 314 (Circuits) — D or better required, cannot be P/F
- Advanced Math (3 cr) + Specialization Elective (3 cr)
- Economics course required in Intellectual Breadth

**Biomedical Engineering**
- BIOMEDE 211, 221, 231, 311, 320, 450
- BIO 172 (or AP Bio credit); MATSCIE 220 or 250
- Technical electives
- AP Biology (4+) satisfies BIO 172

**Civil Engineering**
- CEE 200, 211, 212, 303, 373; program electives (6 of 7 required)
- ECON 101 or 102 in Intellectual Breadth
- C- or better required in all core courses

**Industrial & Operations Engineering**
- IOE 265 (Stats, 4 cr), 310, 316, 333, 366, 373, 430, 436; MDE
- Economics course in Intellectual Breadth

**Aerospace Engineering**
- AERO 205, 215, 225, 335, 347, 405, 415, 440, 481; Advanced Math
- Strong sequence dependency — calc/physics must be on schedule

## School of Nursing (BSN)

4-year direct-entry program. All science and clinical nursing courses must be in-residence. 1,000+ clinical hours.

### Curriculum by Level

| Level | Key Courses | AP/IB Notes |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Level 1 — Year 1 (Science Foundation) | CHEM 125/130 (Gen Chem), BIO 171+172 (Intro Biology), PSYCH 111, FYWR, NURS 218 (Applied Stats), HU/SS electives | AP Chem (4+): satisfies CHEM; AP Bio (4+): satisfies BIO 171+172; AP Psych (4+): satisfies PSYCH 111 |
| Level 2 — Year 2 (Clinical Foundation) | Nursing foundations, health assessment, pathophysiology, pharmacology, first clinical rotations | Must be in-residence |
| Level 3 — Year 3 (Clinical Specialties) | Med-Surg, maternal-child, mental health, community/public health, clinical practicums | Must be in-residence |
| Level 4 — Year 4 (Advanced Practice + Capstone) | Complex acute care, leadership, evidence-based practice, capstone clinical immersion | Must be in-residence |

**Progression rules:**
- Science core (CHEM, BIO, Physiology): C or better to progress
- All nursing clinical courses must be passed to advance to next level
- Sophomore transfer students must complete Level 1 requirements during summer before Year 2
- Transfer students: max 40 credits applied toward BSN

## School of Kinesiology (BS / BA)

### All Kinesiology Students
- 120 credits minimum
- Distribution: NS, SS, HU courses required
- 2.0 GPA overall and in major
- MOVESCI 110 required for ALL students
- Quantitative reasoning course required (STATS 250 or equivalent)

### Movement Science (MOVESCI) — BS
- Core: MOVESCI 230 (Musculoskeletal Anatomy), 250 (Stats), 320 (Motor Behavior), 335 (Exercise Physiology), 355 (Biomechanics), 361 (Sport & Exercise Psych)
- STATS 250 can substitute for MOVESCI 250 if taken before admission to the major
- Anatomy & Physiology with lab (PHYSIOL 201 or equiv): C or better required
- Cognate electives: ~15 upper-level credits (many pre-health students use for grad school prereqs)
- IONM concentration available via additional application

### Applied Exercise Science (AES) — BS
- MATH 105 with C+ or higher required (AP Calc AB or higher satisfies; MATH 115 also accepted)
- Anatomy & Physiology: C or better required
- 15 credits of AES electives required
- No secondary admission application required (unlike MOVESCI/Sport Management)
- Minimum grades in lower-level courses required to progress to upper-level AES

### Sport Management — BA
- Secondary admission application required (apply sophomore or junior year)
- BA degree
- Core in management, marketing, finance, law applied to sport context
- Internship/practicum component required

## IB Credit Notes

| Chemistry HL | 4+ | CHEM 125/126+130 | Same as AP Chemistry |

## Transfer Credit Rules

Transfer credit comes in two types with very different planning implications:

| Type | Meaning | Can Satisfy Requirements? |
|------|---------|--------------------------|
| **Equivalent credit** | Mapped to a specific U-M course (e.g., MATH 115, ECON 101) | YES — satisfies distribution, major prereqs, named requirements |
| **Departmental / general credit** | Subject-area credit without a specific U-M course number | Maybe — often elective buffer only; needs advisor approval |

### College-Specific Transfer Rules
- **LSA:** Transfer credit CAN satisfy Distribution (unlike AP). R&E transfer credit requires petition for credits earned before enrollment.
- **Engineering:** External core math/physics/chem must have CTP status; equivalency evaluation often required before enrolling.
- **Ross:** External coursework must have an existing evaluation in the Transfer Credit Equivalency Database. Business credit is **non-transferable**. Transfer/test credit caps apply (30 or 45 cr by entry type). Must notify bbacrediteval@umich.edu.
- **Nursing:** Science prereq transfer credit accepted; all clinical nursing courses must be in-residence.
- **Kinesiology:** Transfer credit for distribution accepted; major core courses need advisor verification.

**Universal rule:** Request equivalency evaluation BEFORE enrolling in outside coursework if the course is not already in the database.

## Double Majors, Minors & Dual Degrees

### Double Majors Within LSA
- Courses from one major can satisfy requirements of the second where appropriate
- No course satisfying a major requirement can also satisfy the LSA Distribution Requirement
- Both major GPAs must be ≥ 2.0

### Minors Within LSA
- At least 15 credits; at least 9 in-residence
- No course shared between two minors
- At most one course shared between a minor and your major
- No P/F courses in a minor
- AP/IB can satisfy minor prerequisites but NOT minor course requirements

### Dual Degrees (Cross-College)

| Combination | Key Constraint | Typical Credits |
|-------------|---------------|-----------------|
| LSA + Engineering (Combined Program) | Both degree requirements met; courses cross-count where both allow; 10–11 terms typical | ~150–160 cr |
| LSA MDDP (any two colleges) | Both degrees awarded simultaneously; designate a home unit | 150+ cr (30 cr beyond first degree) |
| Engineering + Ross | CoE: one class cannot count toward both a CoE degree and 2+ other programs | ~150+ cr |
| Ross BBA + non-business | Max 15 cr of BBA business credits can be shared toward non-business requirements | ~150 cr; both awarded simultaneously |

| Winter Y2 | FIN 300 or MKT 300 (floating), non-business electives | Begin floating core |

| Winter Y3 | More floating core, language (if not done), non-business elective | Language req |

## Common Pitfalls That Delay Graduation

1. **Credits ≠ Requirements** — Having 120+ credits doesn't mean you've met all named requirements. AP adds credit total but often not Distribution, QR, or Writing. Check Wolverine Access audit constantly.

2. **AP English ≠ First-Year Writing** — AP English Language or Literature (any score) does NOT satisfy FYWR at LSA or ENGR 100 at Engineering. Elective buffer only.

3. **Residency shortfall** — Heavy community college coursework after matriculation can hit the 120-credit total while failing the 60-credit in-residence minimum.

4. **Ross transfer cap trap** — If you enter Ross with 45+ credits, many simply won't count toward the BBA 120 total. Prioritize credits that clear prereqs over generic elective hours.

5. **Engineering C- rule** — A C- in most required engineering/math/science courses is unsatisfactory. You may need to retake it. Unlike LSA where a D passes, Engineering has strict grade floors.

6. **Pass/fail after major declaration (LSA)** — Major/minor courses taken P/F after declaration (post-Fall 2024) will generally NOT count. Don't shift to P/F on a major course mid-degree.

7. **Forgetting ULWR** — Many LSA students do FYWR early and forget ULWR until senior year. Plan it into junior year.

8. **Ross BSTRAT 400 must be final Winter** — Cannot be taken early. If you're thinking of graduating early, this course will constrain your final term.

9. **Ross ILP Milestone 4 is Fall only** — Missing Fall Senior Milestone 4 means you cannot graduate that academic year.

10. **Kinesiology / SMTD secondary admissions** — Sport Management, IONM, and many SMTD programs require a secondary application. Missing the deadline can add a full year.
